Cribbage - Help

Quick Links: Rules | Scoring | Scoring During Play | Options

Cribbage is a card game played in a series of rounds, traditionally with two players, until one player moves a peg around the cribbage board twice (standard cribbage board - 121 points). In this cribbage game, you play the computer instead of a human opponent. Blue is the computer, red is the player.

The game play should be relatively straight forward. When the game starts up, a player can start a new game, set game options or exit. If new game is selected, the cards are shuffled, and 6 cards are dealt to the player and computer. At this point, the first 'crib' holder is drawn for and a dialog displayed to the player on who has the 'crib' at the start. The 6 cards for the player are displayed with a 'Discard' button underneath each card for the player to select two for discard into the 'crib' hand. If 'Discard' is selected, the button text changes to 'Keep'. The 'Keep' allows the player to change their mind about a card chosen for discard. Once two, and only two cards are selected to discard, the 'Continue' button is enabled.

By selecting the 'Continue' button, a player discards the selected cards into the 'crib' hand and game play starts with another card being dealt as an up-card. Note: If during game play start, the up-card dealt is a 'jack', then the 'crib' holder gets two points. The remaining four (4) cards in the players hand are displayed to them with the button text now showing 'Play'. When it is the players turn, they can either choose the 'go' button or one of the 'Play' buttons to play a card. You can only select the 'go' button if you have no play to make (that is, you don't have a card that doesn't take the played cards value tally over 31 points). During game play, if a score is made by either side, a dialog will pop up with a summary of the scores made.

At any time, the 'new game' button can be pressed to start a new game before the current game is completed.

Once the game play is completed and all cards have been played, then scoring begins. Depending on who scores first, the player will see either the score entry dialog or a dialog informing the player what the computer has. With these dialogs, the associated card hand is displayed to the player. If the player holds the 'crib' hand, then those cards and the score entry dialog are shown after the hand score is displayed and entered.

This continues until one player or the other gets to 121 points. At this time (which may happen during any score opportunity), the game wipes all of the cards being displayed off the board and disables all but the 'new game', 'options' and 'exit' buttons. If playing a cribbage match, the winning player will get 1 or more match points. If the winner reaches the winning match point value, they also win the match. If not, a new game should be started to continue the match until one player or the other wins.

Rules

At the start of each round, each player gets six (6) cards of which they will pick two (2) cards to discard into the 'crib' hand leaving them with four (4) cards to play the hand with. After the crib is discarded into, a card is turned up (the rounds up-card) and the round begins with the player holding the 'crib' hand starting by playing one of their four cards. Game play continues with each player taking turns playing a card until all of the cards are played. Note though that during a hand, you may not be able to play a card because the total of the cards played makes more than 31 points. In which case the player who played last card gets one point and the played cards are cleared from the table, resetting the tally. During game play, it is possible to make other scores.

After all of the cards are played from both players hands, the hands are then scored starting with the player who does not hold the 'crib' hand. The other player then scores their hand and the 'crib' hand. At any point during this scoring round if one player or the other makes 121 points, the game is over. When scoring the hands at this stage, the up-card is included within the hand as an additional card. New hands are dealt to both players if neither has reached 121 points starting a new round with the other player getting the 'crib' hand.

If the 'match points' option is checked, a cribbage match is played (typically 3, 5 or 7 match points) which could be double the match points of games or less if skunk points are won. Additional match point(s) can be gotten by 'skunking' the opponent. A skunk is counted if the losing player has less than 91 points when the winning player gets to 121. If not playing with the match points option turned on, skunks are ignored. With the 'match points' option, the winning number of match points can be set and the game will let the player know once either they or the computer has won the match. It is up to the player to keep starting new games until the match has been won. Double and triple skunk points can be scored if the option for 'double/triple skunks scored' is checked. A double skunk is scored with 3 additional match points if the losing player has less than 61 points. A triple skunk is scored with 7 additional match points if the losing player has less than 31 points. A triple skunk will be an automatic win for a standard match played to 7 points or less.

  • Play Hints:
  • When determining what cards to discard, you should keep the hand that without the up-card gives the biggest score possible. All that the up-card can do then is make your score better. Sometimes you can get lucky guessing what the up-card might be, but overall, you should keep the hand that will give the biggest return not depending on the up-card.
  • If at all possible, do not play a five as your first card. It is too easy for the opponent to make 15 when a five is played first.
  • When determining what card to play next, play the card that will give you points. If not, try to play a card that will not give a point advantage to the opponent.

Scoring

Scoring a cribbage hand is not difficult, though it is possible to miss points. Note that there are some differences to the hand scores depending on if a players hand is being scored or the 'crib' hand is being scored. The following table contains the combinations and the scores possible by each one (note for point values, face cards count as 10 points).

Jack (of up-card suit) 1 point
15 points (in any unique combination of cards - 2, 3, 4 or 5) 2 points
Flush (all same suit)
- Note: if "crib" hand, all cards including the up-card must be of the same suit. 		4 points/5 if up-card same suit also or crib hand
Pair 2 points
Three-of-a-kind 6 points (3 pairs)
Four-of-a-kind 12 points (6 pairs)
Unique Straights (3 cards and over) 3, 4 or 5 points depending on the number of cards.
  • Score Examples:
  • Hand: 7S, 7C, 8S, 9D - up-card 1H = 14 points (2 3 card straights, 1 pair, 3 sets of 15 (7S/8S 7C/8S 7S/7C/1H))
  • Hand: 5D, 5S, JS, QD - up-card QS = 17 points (jack of suit, 2 pairs, 6 sets of 15)
  • Crib Hand: 6D, 9D, 10D, QD - up-card 5H = 6 points (3 sets of 15 - crib hand needs up-card of suit to count flush)
  • Other Items:
  • Note: it is possible (bad luck) to have a hand that has no points in it. There is a better chance that the 'crib' hand may have no points in it.
  • If points are missed, the computer will call you on it and score (for itself) the difference. This is known as 'Muggins'. The computer will not miss points so the player will not have this opportunity.
Scoring During Play

Scores that can be gotten during game play are similar to hand scoring. The following table illustrates the scores that can be had. More than one of these could possibly be gotten and if so, then multiple dialogs will display the different items that made scores. Note: for point scores, face cards (jacks, queens, kings) count as 10 points. Also note, the up-card is not used for scoring during game play.

The possible scores are:
Jack (dealt as up-card, for "crib" holder) 2 points
15 points (tally of cards played) 2 points
31 points (tally of cards played) 2 points
Pair 2 points
Three-of-a-kind 6 points
Four-of-a-kind 12 points
Straight (3 + cards). The straight does not have to be played in order (exp: 5S, 7D, 6C, 4D - first three gave first player 3 points, playing the fourth gave second player 4 points). Between 3 and number of cards in straight (maximum would be 8 points).
Last card played, unless 31 points made 1 point

If a player gets blocked after the other player has run out of cards, then the player does not get to count last point (self-blocked).

For more information on Cribbage and Cribbage rules, see the Cribbage entry on Wikipedia.

Options

There are a few options that can be set by the player. Options are set by selecting the 'options' button, then setting the option desired in the dialog. Press OK to save, cancel to quit without saving the options.

  • Prompts On - If checked, turns on a yes/no prompt if the new game button or exit button is selected and current game isn't over yet. Also prompts if match play is on and the current match has not been won yet when the exit button has been pushed.
  • Human Player Always Starts - If checked, the crib hand is always given to the player first, never the computer.
  • Crib Board Sounds On - If checked, peg moves make a clicking sound when being moved within the cribbage board.
  • Card Back Image - Select an image to display when a card back image is needed (the up-card position). The image selected is displayed for preview within the dialog. Only one of the images in the list can be selected.
  • Match Points - Instead of 'Games Won' showing, 'Match Points' is displayed. Also, match points allow for the skunk rule to give an additional match point (or multiple if double/triple skunk turned on).
  • Match Points for Win - Number of match points needed to win the match. Value could be from 3 - 99 and should be an odd value. Traditionally, matches are to 3, 5, or 7 points. This options is only available and used if the Match Points options is checked.
  • Double/Triple Skunk Scored - Double and triple skunks are scored during match play. See the rules for what constitutes double and triple skunks. Regular match play does not typically score double and triple skunk points. For this game, double skunk is an additional 3 match points, triple skunk is an additional 7 match points. Triple skunk should be an automatic win for matches played to 7 points or less.
